## Chemistry You Can See and Touch – Right in Your Browser

**Zperiod** is not just a digital version of the traditional periodic table – it's a complete interactive educational environment, combining 3D atomic models, advanced chemistry tools, a simple virtual lab, and a smart worksheet generator.

🔗 **Official Website:** [zperiod.app](https://zperiod.app) – Available in multiple languages (English, Arabic, Spanish, French, and more)

---

## What Does Zperiod Offer?

### ⚛️ Complete Interactive Periodic Table
- **118 chemical elements** with detailed data (atomic number, atomic mass, electron configuration, valences, electronegativity, and more)
- **3D atom models** for every element – rotate and zoom to see electrons and nucleus
- **Ions tab** – view common ions for each element with their charges
- **Flexible filtering** – filter elements by group, period, state (solid/liquid/gas), or classification (metals, nonmetals, transition metals…)

### 🧪 Integrated Chemistry Tools (Grades 9-12)

| Tool | Function |
|------|----------|
| **Equation Balancer** | Automatically balance complex chemical equations with visual stoichiometry feedback |
| **Molar Mass** | Instantly calculate molecular weights with a detailed element-by-element mass breakdown |
| **Solubility Table** | Interactive charts and rules for ionic compound solubilities – quick reference while solving problems |
| **Virtual Lab** | Simple lab scene where you can tilt a beaker and interact with water particles – basic reaction simulation |

### 📝 Smart Worksheet Generator
- Generates practice questions from **real, commonly taught reactions**
- Perfect for teachers preparing quick class exercises or students self-practicing
- One click: "Generate Worksheet" – get new problems in seconds

### 🌍 Flexible Global Units
You can change the global units (temperature, density, energy) according to your country's system (SI, Imperial, etc.)

## How Teachers Use Zperiod in the Classroom

### Scenario 1: Explaining Atomic Structure
- Open the periodic table, choose an element (e.g., carbon)
- Rotate and zoom the 3D atom model with touch or mouse
- Discuss with students: Where are the electrons? What does the nucleus look like?

### Scenario 2: Balancing Chemical Equations
- Enter an unbalanced equation like `H2 + O2 -> H2O`
- Click "Equation Balancer" – students see the balancing steps with visual explanation
- Compare their manual method with the automated result

### Scenario 3: Quick Practice – Molar Mass and Solubility
- Assign students a random compound (e.g., `Na2SO4`)
- Ask them to calculate the molar mass manually, then verify using the Molar Mass tool
- For ionic compounds, check the solubility table to see if it precipitates in water

### Scenario 4: Instant Worksheet
- Click "Worksheet Generator" → select difficulty level or topic → click "Generate"
- Print or distribute the worksheet digitally to students within one minute
